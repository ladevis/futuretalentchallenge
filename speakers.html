Jarek Sacharski

Voice Flow

A leader’s voice is a man’s voice - cultural preconceptions about the human voice

TALK

Where does this stereotype come from, how can we change this cultural bias and empower women to regain their right to speak and authority through voice training?

WORKSHOP

Voice and Body in Communication

The human voice is the main tool of everyday communication which significantly influences our interactions with people.

Each of us is given a natural voice, one that is free, strong and spontaneous in expressing emotions. In the process of upbringing this “natural voice” is influenced by culture, education and social norms, therefore losing its ability to express emotions and feelings. Only by becoming aware of what is habitual and what is natural, one can rediscover the freedom and power of their voice.

During a 3 hour workshop, we will explore how simple physical exercises can release our voices from unnecessary tensions. We will also practice stress-free public speaking and find ways to connect better with others through voice and presence.

BIO

Jarek Sacharski is a Voice and Public Speaking Coach based in Amsterdam. His main passion is an exploration of human ‘presence’ and working with individuals on regaining their natural voices through vocational courses.

He coaches TEDx speakers, board members and executives of top 500 companies incl. Facebook, Toyota, PWC and Heineken. He is also a lecturer at the University of West London, Guildhall School of Music and Drama, Warsaw Academy of Dramatic Arts, and University of Social Science and Humanities in Warsaw.

Jarek cooperates with Annos Africa charity on youth educational art projects in Kenya and Malawi.

Bas Raijmakers

STBY - Design Academy Eindhoven

Systemic Sustainable Design through Service Design

TALK

coming soon..

BIO

Bas Raijmakers is creative director at STBY, and along with Geke, one of our founders. Like all of our senior design researchers, Bas has been involved with a wide range of different service design projects. He is particularly involved with much of our video work, having developed the ‘Design Documentary’ format himself in his PhD thesis. Bas is a frequent contributor to academic and industry conferences (you can find some of his articles in the publications section), and has delivered keynote addresses at service design conferences around the world.

Bas has been working in the internet and media industry as a consultant and research manager since the early nineties. Together with Geke, he co-founded ACS-i media research projects in 1993. After the company grew to a 25 people enterprise, it was acquired by Lost Boys International in Amsterdam, whom Bas joined as Usability Director.

STBY arose at least in part from Bas’ fascination with how people use, understand and appreciate media in contemporary popular culture. Since the rise of public internet his focus has been on new media, ranging from hacker cultures to more mainstream uses of the internet. In his many research projects he has provided evidence that it is the people who use new media offerings, not seldom in unanticipated ways, determine the true meaning and value of it.

Bas holds an MA in Communication Sciences from the University of Amsterdam (1990) and a PhD degree in Design Interactions from the Royal College of Art in London (2007). He splits his time between STBY’s London and Amsterdam offices, and in addition to that he is a part-time Reader in Strategic Creativity at Design Academy Eindhoven where he leads a team of graduated design researchers in academic design research projects.

Ad Hessels

Werk is Theater

At the scene' - videomaking as it happens

WORKSHOP

Now and surely in the future we communicate more via images and sound, rather than text. The youth develops that talent on a daily basis with a smart phone. And now that everyone had a good camera with on your smart-phone, you are constantly 'at the scene', the unique opportunity for video content. And with more sense of frame, storyline and sound, the videos look much more professional. In the workshop I share my experience in making videos. The subjects are framing, lighting, story-line, sound, voice-over, interview technique, script, vlog, zoom, editing and a lot of creativity. The future skill. Make your unique video in the workshop and start recording in the field.

BIO

Werk is theater. And we give each business its own stage for an irresistible performance. A performance set by the infectious enthusiasm of a close-knit team that wants to win the business game and wants to present itself as an organization with promotional videos.

Ad Heessels started working with actor Ad Fernhout in 2001 the company Werk is theater (werkistheater.nl) with the dream that companies create an experience that makes you irresistible to customers from an infectious enthusiasm. Ad graduated from the Hogeschool Utrecht with the direction of sustainable energy. Then 20 years of experience as a project and account manager. And since 2001 mainly active in interactive workshops, teambuilding and meetings. In 2010 he discovered the camera as a stage and combines his interview skills with filmmaking. In 2017 with Ad Fernhout started production of the online TV series De Goede Hoop (degoedehoop.online)
